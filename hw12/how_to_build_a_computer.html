Building a Computer

Depending on what your intrest level is building a computer can be a difficult thing. You might not even know where to start. I wanted make something to get you started on what could be a new favorit past time.

The first thing you'll need to start with is figuring out what you want the computer to be used for, gaming, work, editing photos. What you choose will decide a lot of the components you'll want to get. As well as the price range you'll find yourself in.

A good place to start doing some research on what price and hardware you'll need for the kind of PC you want to build. User benchmark is a great place to start. Another great resource is this article by Michael Crider. It goes into more depth than this page and will help you get a better understanding of everything you need in your shiny new computer.

What Do You Need to Start?

Basic Hardware
Hardware Name Why you need it How much they usually cost
Motherboard This is what everything will connect to on your computer. This is like the head of your computer. It also houses the brain $150 - $500
CPU or Processor This is the brain of your computer. It tells every other part of your computer what it should be doing and how to do it. $150 - $700
Graphics Card This is the muscle of your computer. It's also what you'll probably be bragging to your friends or co-workers about. $230 - $1200
Tower Well you need something to keep everything in, might as well make it look nice. $100 - $800
RAM You'll need this so you can access things faster and not take all day trying to remember what you were doing a second ago. $50 - $400
Power Supply This one is really elictrical, so make sure you get something that can handle the pressure. $250 - $950
